Addressing Disproportionate Gun Possession Charges among Young Black Men in Englewood

Built an analytical pipeline to expose racial disparities in arrests, felony review, and bond outcomes.

Tools: R (Tidyverse, ggplot2)
Client: Teamwork Englewood

Overview#

As Project Manager, I led a five-person volunteer team that analyzed Chicago crime data to identify trends in first-time gun possession cases, particularly looking at first-time gun possession cases among young Black men in Englewood. The project focused specifically on the experiences of young Black men in the Englewood neighborhood to support data-driven advocacy. I architected an analytical pipeline in R to load and integrate multiple datasets and generate time-series visualizations, surfacing critical trends in felony review and bond outcomes. These tools, in conjunction with policy materials produced by my teammates, directly support the client’s advocacy efforts following the elimination of cash bail.

My Contributions#

  • Data Synthesis: Cleaned and joined disparate datasets from Cook County Government Open Data and City of Chicago Data Portal focused on arrests, initiations, and diversions.
  • Data Visualization: Developed clear, high-impact visualizations using ggplot2 to communicate disparities and trends in arrests, felony review, and bond outcomes to non-technical stakeholders.
  • Project Leadership: Translated stakeholder objectives into a technical roadmap and coordinated team efforts, including managing timelines and reviewing all deliverables.
