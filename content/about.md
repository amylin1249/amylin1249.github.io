Hi! I'm Amy (she/her). I'm a first-year graduate student passionate about leveraging data and technology to advance social justice. I focus on applying technical skills to domestic social policy issues, including criminal justice and affordable housing, and aspire to work in nonprofit or civic tech. Having had the privilege of living in four major regions of the United States (I grew up in California, went to college in Texas, worked in New York, and now live and study in Illinois), I’m particularly drawn to local policy work and urban issues.

Currently, I'm pursuing a Master of Science degree in Computational Analysis and Public Policy (MSCAPP) at the University of Chicago. Previously, I worked in health policy research and advocacy. I hold a Bachelor of Arts from Rice University in Computational and Applied Math; the Study of Women, Gender, and Sexuality; and Spanish. 

In my free time, I love baking, hiking, and discovering new music. I’m always excited to learn from others, share ideas, and collaborate on projects, so please feel free to [get in touch](/contact/)!
