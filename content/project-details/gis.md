### Overview
Working with a teammate, we developed a spatial analysis model to identify Chicago census tracts most at risk of gentrification. Rather than relying on a single indicator, we implemented a composite indexing framework that synthesized market pressure, demographic change, and housing instability to flag high-risk areas. The resulting spatial tools enable policymakers to identify vulnerable zones and implement targeted housing interventions before displacement occurs.

### My Contributions
* **Methodological Framework:** Led the research to establish three distinct metric-based definitions of “gentrification risk," ensuring the model accounted for diverse policy lenses, such as housing cost burdens versus demographic shifts.
* **Data Strategy & Sourcing:** Identified and integrated non-traditional data sources, including eviction filings and building permit data, to supplement standard Census metrics, providing a more real-time view of neighborhood change.
* **Attribute Data Engineering**: Executed the attribute field calculations and data normalization within ArcGIS to convert raw metrics into standardized scores for the composite index.
